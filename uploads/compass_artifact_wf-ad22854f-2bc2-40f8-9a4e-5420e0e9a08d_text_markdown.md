## Key Findings

**The macro is exceptionally favorable for a telecom–AI bridge play in 2026.** TeleGeography (Lane Burdette & Paul Brodsky, "What to Know About Transport Networks in 2026") forecasts "nearly 40 cables come online" in 2026, "a capital expenditure of roughly $6 billion, which is the highest number seen in at least a decade," and announced subsea capex between 2026–2029 exceeds $16B (Brodsky). LATAM data-center investment grew at 42% annually 2022–2025 (datacenterHawk 2026 LATAM Hyperscale & Infrastructure Outlook), with Brazil, Mexico, Querétaro, Chile and Colombia all in active hyperscale build cycles (White & Case, 2026). Miami sits at the seam: the NAP of the Americas, ~62,000 miles of submarine cabling into LATAM/Caribbean/Europe, and per the Miami-Dade Beacon Council, in 2023 the Miami Customs District achieved a total trade value of $137 billion — about 45% of all US-LATAM trade (with 2024 trade rising to $144B per Global Miami's "State of Trade 2025"). Dante is literally on the geographic seam where this capex lands.

**Yet the AI layer in LATAM telecom is wide open.** Per the IBM/TM Forum/Oxford Economics "Navigating Autonomous Networks" study (published September 8, 2025), "only 19% have integrated AI into three or more network or OSS functions, which is where most closed-loop systems reside." Vivo Brazil is publicly piloting Ericsson's Agentic rApp-as-a-Service on AWS (TelecomTV, Feb 2026), and O2 Telefónica + Tech Mahindra + NVIDIA are building the "Large Telco Model" (RCR Wireless, Sept 2, 2025). Aduna is commercializing CAMARA-compliant network APIs with Vivo, Claro and TIM Brasil targeting Q4 2025 launch. But Tier-2/3 LATAM operators (Cirion, IFX Networks, ufinet, ETB, Algar, Megacable, Entel) have made essentially zero public agentic-AI announcements. That's the white space Dante can credibly attack.

**The pricing environment is bifurcated and favorable to a boutique.** US AI consulting rates in 2026 sit at $150–$500/hour, with boutique senior practitioners commanding $300–$400/hour and monthly retainers of $10K–$25K typical, climbing to $50K–$150K for enterprise-scale engagements (Groovy Web, "AI Consulting Rates 2026"; Codewave, 2026; Nicola Lazzari, 2026). Fractional CTO retainers run $5K–$25K/month (CTO.la 2025). Meanwhile, per TeleGeography's "Key telecom trends in Brazil" Q2 2025 dataset, the Miami–São Paulo 100 Gbps weighted-median wholesale price has fallen to $10,400 — with quotes "as low as $5,000 for 100 Gbps on this main route" — an 18% CAGR price compression 2022–2025. Transport is cheap and abundant; the value has shifted to the *integration, design, automation, and operational expertise layer* — exactly where Dante's 28 years sit.

**Critical structural finding: no funded "Lightyear-for-LATAM" exists.** Per PR Newswire (November 19, 2024), "Lightyear, the leading provider of enterprise telecom management software, today announces the completion of its $31 million Series B fundraise led by Altos Ventures, with meaningful participation from existing investors Ridge Ventures, Amplo, Zigg Capital, and Susa Ventures" — plus a $16M extension filing in February 2026 (Tracxn). But Lightyear operates US-only. There is no comparable LATAM enterprise telecom procurement platform with disclosed VC funding. This is an authentic white-space opportunity, though one that's hard to bootstrap to scale solo.

## Caveats

1. **Macro execution risk in LATAM is real.** Mexico's regulatory clarity remains uneven; Argentina's currency volatility complicates contract pricing; Brazil's data sovereignty rules (LGPD) and Anatel's permitting timelines are genuine project-delay drivers. Price all engagements in USD with quarterly FX-adjustment clauses for LATAM-headquartered customers.

2. **The submarine-cable capex narrative ($16B 2026–2029) is concentrated in trans-Pacific and trans-Atlantic content-provider builds.** Pure LATAM intra-region investment is only ~$200M–$1.2B over the same period (TeleGeography), and capacity prices on key US-LATAM routes have fallen 18–23% CAGR 2022–2025. Dante's value is in operations and integration — not in trying to participate in cable building.

3. **Agentic AI in telecom remains hype-heavy.** Rakuten Symphony, TM Forum, NVIDIA, and Microsoft are publishing significant amounts of architectural marketing; real production deployments at full autonomy are still rare. Far EasTone's 60% AI-assisted NOC is the leading published case (Microsoft, Feb 24, 2026). Sell augmentation, not replacement.

4. **Florida's "no state income tax" advantage is real but offset by hurricane risk, healthcare costs as a self-employed founder, and the need to pay both halves of self-employment tax (15.3%) federally.** Budget ~30–35% effective federal tax on consulting income; consider an S-corp election once consistent income exceeds ~$80K/year.

5. **Miami VC capital is real but relationship-driven.** Fuel Venture Capital, The Venture City, Rokk3r, Florida Funders rarely take cold meetings — the eMerge Americas circuit (April 23–24, 2026) and Refresh Miami events are essential for any future fundraise. Per Pitchbook, South Florida startups raised $1.15B in Q1 2026, but the median deal is fintech/healthtech/proptech — telecom-AI is a niche pitch that needs strong sector framing.

6. **Recently laid off in January 2026** can be reframed in pitches as "founded Monkyfi in early 2026 after 28 years at Telefónica/Telxius" — never lead with the layoff. Use the Q1 2026 timing as the founding story.

7. **The most likely failure mode is over-diversification.** Solo founders who attempt 3+ product lines in year one typically ship none well. The discipline is: #1 funds everything; #3 is a single SaaS shipped in evenings; #2 is sales motion only, no product build, until Q4. Resist building #4 or #5 in 2026.
